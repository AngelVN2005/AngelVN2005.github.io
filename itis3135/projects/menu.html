Appetizers

Tostones Rellenos

Crispy fried plantain cups filled with your choice of shrimp al ajillo or ropa vieja, a crowd-pleasing appetizer.

Quipes

Deep-fried bulgur wheat rolls typically stuffed with savory seasoned ground beef.

Pastelitos

Crispy turnover pastries filled with cheese, chicken, or beef.

Yuca Frita

Fried cassava sticks or cassava fritters.

Yaniqueques

Crispy, salty dough fritters.

Croquetas de Pollo

Creamy chicken croquettes.

Main Dishes

Sancocho Dominicano

A hearty seven-meat stew slow-cooked with root vegetables and fragrant herbs, the ultimate Dominican comfort food.

La Bandera

Meaning "The Flag," this is the most traditional lunch, white rice, red beans, and stewed meat, usually served with salad and avocado.

Mofongo

Fried green plantains mashed with garlic, olive oil, and pork rinds, commonly served with broth or topped with shrimp.

Locrio

A rice-based dish similar to paella, featuring rice cooked with tomato sauce and meats like chicken, salami, or sardines.

Pastelón de Plátano Maduro

A sweet and savory casserole, often called a "Dominican lasagna," made with layered sweet plantains and ground beef.

Asopao

A hearty, soupy rice dish often prepared with chicken, seafood, or pork.

Desserts

Tres Leches Casero

Our homestyle tres leches cake soaked overnight for maximum flavor, topped with fresh cream and seasonal fruit.

Habichuelas con Dulce

A unique Easter favorite made of pureed red kidney beans, coconut milk, evaporated milk, spices, raisins, and sweet potato chunks.

Bizcocho Dominicano

A light, moist pound cake typically filled with pineapple jam and topped with meringue icing.

Flan

Creamy caramel custard, often made with coconut milk and condensed milk.

Arroz con Leche

A creamy and aromatic dessert prepared with rice, whole milk, evaporated milk, condensed milk, cinnamon, cloves, and a touch of lemon zest.

Pudín de Pan

Bread pudding, often served with a dulce de leche sauce.
